## üí°Design Sprint Brief

### Sprint Brief

- a document the you share with all your attendees to help them prepare for the sprint

### DESIGN SPRINT BRIEF

SPRINT CHALLENGE: Build exciting new features for the cycling app in time for relaunch later in the year

What is the challenge that you want to solve in the sprint?

Here are four things that make a great challenge:

- The challenge is something **real** that the team needs to deliver
- It's stated in a way that sounds **inspiring** - something to solve for
- It's **clear and concise**
- It includes a **time frame** (next quarter? 3 years from now?)

Example: Build an exciting new feature for a cycling app in time for relaunch later in the year.

---

### KEY DELIVERABLES: ‚Ä¶‚Ä¶‚Ä¶

What do you want the team to create during the sprint?

A few tips for deliverables:

- Aim for the highest quality deliverables possible. Polished digital work, videos, and interactive prototypes are better than sketches.
- List all platforms that need to be designed for - e.g., web, mobile, tablet / physical product + website / environment.

Example: Complete prototype that includes the feature update. Or user journey flows for X and Y.

### RESOURCES:

For short term sprints: Assignment development team, if any:

It's recommended that you start a design sprint by having assigned development resources to carry the work after the sprint. This is easier in the case of short-term focused sprints.

For long-term / vision sprints: Plan to secure resources:

Vision sprints take a long-term view of planning. In order to succeed, our team needs to have a plan of approvals for how to integrate the sprint within the organizational roadmap.

4. Estimated launch plan

When is the projected launch for the piece we are designing?

What is this likely to look like at launch - e.g., a website, campaign, service, physical product. Be sure to list that in the challenge statement as well.

## üß© Entry-Level Designer‚Äôs Role in a Design Sprint

**Purpose:** Support the team while learning the sprint process and contributing to design tasks.

**Key Responsibilities:**

- **Assist in research:** Take notes during interviews, organize user feedback, and help gather insights.
- **Support ideation:** Contribute ideas during brainstorming and sketching sessions.
- **Create visual assets:** Help produce wireframes, mockups, icons, or other design elements.
- **Document the process:** Record decisions, capture sketches, and maintain sprint materials.
- **Collaborate:** Work closely with senior designers, UX researchers, and engineers.
- **Prototype support:** Assist in building clickable prototypes or simple interactive demos.
- **Observe & learn:** Pay attention to sprint facilitation, decision-making, and cross-functional collaboration.

**Key Skills Gained:**

- Understanding of the sprint process end-to-end
- Exposure to user testing and feedback interpretation
- Experience working in a cross-functional team
- Hands-on practice with design tools and prototyping

## üîÑ Sprint Retrospective

**Meaning:**

A sprint retrospective is a structured meeting held at the end of a sprint where the team reflects on their work process, discusses what went well, identifies challenges, and decides on improvements for future sprints.

**Purpose:**

- Improve team efficiency and collaboration
- Celebrate successes and acknowledge achievements
- Identify obstacles and ways to overcome them
- Continuously refine processes for better outcomes

**Key Questions Typically Asked:**

- What went well during the sprint?
- What didn‚Äôt go well or could be improved?
- What actions can we take to improve the next sprint?

**Outcome:**

A list of actionable improvements to make future sprints more effective and productive.
