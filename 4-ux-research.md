## Choosing the Right Research Method

### **1️⃣ Primary Research**

- **Definition:** Collecting original data directly from users.
- **Purpose:** Understand user behavior, needs, and validate ideas.
- **Methods / Examples:**
    - User interviews
    - Usability testing
    - Surveys / questionnaires
    - Field studies / observation

### **2️⃣ Secondary Research**

- **Definition:** Using existing data or research without collecting new data.
- **Purpose:** Understand market trends, competitors, and context.
- **Methods / Examples:**
    - Competitor analysis
    - Market reports / case studies
    - Analytics review
    - Academic / online resources

### **1️⃣ Qualitative Data**

- **Definition:** Non-numerical data that explains **why** users behave a certain way.
- **Purpose:** Understand motivations, feelings, pain points, and experiences.
- **Methods:** User interviews, usability testing, observations, diary studies

### **2️⃣ Quantitative Data**

- **Definition:** Numerical data that shows **what, how much, or how often**.
- **Purpose:** Identify patterns, trends, and measurable metrics.
- **Methods:** Surveys, analytics, A/B testing, heatmaps
