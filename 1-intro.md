## Jobs in the field

**Interaction designers :** Focus on designing the experience of a product and how it functions

**Visual designers :** How a product or technology looks ( designing logo or icons font)

**Motion designers :** Think about what it feels like for a user to move through a product 

**Graphic designer :** Create visuals that tells a story or message

**UX designer** : How user interact with a product

**UX researcher** : Conduct studies or interviews that help us learn how people use a product

**UX writers :** Think about about how to make the language within a product clearer

**Production designers :** Make sure first and finest designer match in the finished project materials and that the assets are ready to be handed off to the engineering team

**Assets :** Everything from text and images to the design specifications like font style, color, size and spacing

**UX engineers** : Translate the design’s intent into a functioning experience

**UX program managers :** Ensure clear and timely communication so that the process of building a useful product moves smoothly from start to finish (setting up goals and writing project plans

## Type of Designers

### 1. Generalist Designer

**What It Is:** A designer with broad skills across multiple design areas such as UI, UX, branding, illustration, and content.

**Benefit:** Provides flexibility and can cover many design needs, making them valuable for small teams or fast-moving projects.

**How the Role Works:** They switch between different design tasks based on project demands, filling gaps and supporting various stages of the design process.

---

### 2. Specialist Designer

**What It Is:** A designer who focuses deeply on one specific discipline (e.g., UX research, UI design, motion, illustration, typography).

**Benefit:** Delivers expert-level quality and precision in their chosen specialty, ideal for complex or high-detail work.

**How the Role Works:** They concentrate on tasks within their specialty and collaborate with other designers to integrate their expertise into the full product experience.

---

### 3. T-Shaped Designer

**What It Is:** A designer who has broad knowledge across many areas (like a generalist) but also deep expertise in one core specialty.

**Benefit:** Balances flexibility with strong craft, enabling them to collaborate broadly while owning a specialized skill area.

**How the Role Works:** They contribute across the design workflow, leveraging their general knowledge, while taking lead responsibility for the part of the project tied to their deep specialty.

## Working with cross functional team

How these cross functional team work together to build an project?

example : cross functional teams to build an app for ordering food : 

- **Product lead** : Decides the scope of the project
- **UX researcher** : Tries to understand user trust
- **Interaction designer** : Figures out the flow for ordering
- **Visual designer** : Thinks about how to make it easy for users to find allergen free food in the product
- Engineers : Brings wireframes and prototypes to life
- Program manager : Ensure clear and timely communication across the team

## Understand design sprint

The goal of the design sprint is to solve a critical design challenge through designing prototyping and testing ideas with users.

**Benefit:**

- Speeds up decision-making and innovation.
- Validates ideas early with users.
- Encourages collaboration across team

**How it works:**

A typical design sprint lasts 4–5 days and follows a sequence of phases, from understanding the problem to testing a prototype with users.
